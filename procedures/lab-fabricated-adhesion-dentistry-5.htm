Lab Fabricated Adhesion Dentistry:
Adhesion Fixed Bridges

The concept of adhesion dentistry has quite naturally expanded to fulfill the demand of fixed bridgework but on a limited basis.

If the abutment teeth are free of both decay and previously placed dental restorations, the patients may be a candidate for noninvasive adhesion short-span porcelain/composite or porcelain-fused-to-metal adhesion bridges.

The following photographs are example of adhesion bridgework in porcelain/composite and porcelain-fused-metal.

Patient's concern: function, realistic cosmetic results, and invasion of adjacent healthy teeth.

LAB-FABRICATED ADHESION DENTISTRY: ADHESION FIXED BRIDGES - PORCELAIN FUSED-TO-METAL

This case demonstrates a " Maryland bridge", where the missing tooth is replaced with porcelain fused to the metal framework. The framework is then adhered to the abutment teeth.

This technique has limited application, and the greatest success when used for single tooth replacement in low-leveraged area of the mouth. (Low-leveraged area of the mouth are toward the front teeth, while high level-leveraged area are toward the back molars.)
