The Amalgam (Silver Filling) "Controversy":

Replacement with Pre-Capsulated "Bonded" Amalgam

This photo represents a failing "silver filling" originally placed with in the limits of the restorative material. Like any thing else, these materials fatigue and wear out.

The following pages describe re-treatment with precapsulated (bonded*) amalgam and other genuine treatment alternatives currently available.

 

*Amalgam can now be placed with adhesive agent.

The replacement of failing amalgam restorations with a pre-capsulated amalgam restoration has a success record that cannot be denied.

Advantage: conservation of tooth structure, placement of a decay-resistant material, low cost, and the historical success of this material over the last 150 years.

Disadvantages: cosmetically poor, non-adhesive material* and the possible toxicity ("controversial") of the material.

*Amalgam can now be placed with adhesive agent.
