The Amalgam (Silver Filling) "Controversy":
Replacement with Quartz Filled Composite Resin

The Replacement of Amalgam with Composite Restorative Materials

Although these quartz-filled resin materials are technically difficult to manage, they offer the brightest future for the simple restorative needs of most patients. Greatest advantages are: function, durability, cosmetics, and low cost. Almost every year these composite materials continue to improve to better moot the needs of our patients.

Replacement with Porcelain or Composite Resin

Replacement of Amalgam with Porcelain or Laboratory-cured Composite Materials

This treatment alternative involves custom inlays of porcelain or composite that are permanently "bonded" into the tooth defect. Advantages are: function, durability and cosmetics. The major disadvantages is cost.
